
PLATFORM PRIVACY POLICY
1. INTRODUCTION AND APPLICABILITY
This Privacy Notice sets forth the data processing practices, privacy obligations, and statutory disclosures of Medalverse Co., Ltd. (the "Company") regarding the collection, utilization, and safeguarding of personally identifiable information within the educational technology platform and artificial intelligence services (the "Services").
To ensure strict compliance with the Personal Data Protection Act of Thailand (PDPA) and applicable data protection laws, the processing activities of the Company are classified into two distinct legal capacities:
Capacity as Data Controller: In instances where a natural person (the "Data Subject") directly registers an account, manages a digital portfolio, or explicitly utilizes the algorithmic profile tools, the Company operates as the Data Controller regarding the foundational account information.
Capacity as Data Processor: In instances where an educational institution, corporate partner, or event host (the "Organizer") issues digital certificates or uploads event attendance records, the Organizer retains the exclusive status of Data Controller. In such operations, the Company acts strictly as a Data Processor, executing storage and hosting functions solely upon the instruction of the Organizer.
2. PROTECTION OF MINORS
2.1. Age Requirements. The Services provided by the Company are not inherently directed toward individuals under the legal age of consent as defined by the Personal Data Protection Act of Thailand (PDPA) and applicable data protection laws.
2.2. Parental Consent and Organizer Liability. In instances where the Organizer utilizes the platform infrastructure to issue digital credentials to Data Subjects who have not attained the legal age of majority, the Organizer assumes strict and absolute liability for securing the requisite, verifiable parental or guardian consent prior to transmitting such Personal Data to the Company. The Company reserves the right to promptly purge any Personal Data associated with a minor upon discovery if the requisite statutory consent has not been lawfully obtained and documented by the Organizer.
3. CATEGORIES OF PERSONAL DATA COLLECTED
The Company systematically collects and processes the following categories of Personal Data through the provision of the Services:
3.1. Data Subject Submissions (Account Data): Information provided directly by the Data Subject upon account registration, explicitly including legal names, electronic mail addresses, cryptographic passwords, and any professional or academic details voluntarily entered into the public profile architecture.
3.2. Organizer Transmissions (Event Data): Information transmitted to the platform infrastructure by an Organizer for the purpose of credential issuance. This category includes academic records, event attendance logs, digital certificates, and event-related multimedia.
3.3. Automated Telemetry and Tracking Technologies (System Data): Technical data automatically captured during interaction with the platform infrastructure. This includes Internet Protocol (IP) addresses, browser classification, device identification metrics, navigational analytics, and data collected through cookies, web beacons, or similar tracking technologies. Such tracking mechanisms are strictly utilized to ensure system security, maintain user session integrity, and optimize operational performance.
4. PURPOSES OF DATA PROCESSING
The Company shall process the collected Personal Data strictly for the following authorized operational purposes:
4.1. Service Execution: To host digital certificates, maintain secure digital credential portfolios, and facilitate the authorized sharing of educational achievements.
4.2. Algorithmic Processing: To analyze academic records and platform activity utilizing proprietary and third-party artificial intelligence tools for the specific purpose of synthesizing skills and generating professional resumes for the Data Subject.
4.3. Administrative Communication: To transmit essential system notifications, security alerts, technical support directives, and updates regarding the issuance of new digital certificates.
4.4. Security and Compliance: To actively monitor against unauthorized access attempts, mitigate fraudulent activity, and maintain the overarching technical integrity of the cloud infrastructure.
4.5. Marketing and Promotional Communications: To transmit promotional materials, platform updates, or service-related marketing communications. The Company shall only transmit such communications where the Data Subject has provided explicit, opt-in consent. The Data Subject retains the absolute right to withdraw consent and opt out of receiving promotional communications at any time via the automated unsubscribe mechanisms provided within the communication or through the account settings within the platform interface.
5. PHOTOGRAPHIC MEDIA AND EVENT DOCUMENTATION
5.1. Processing Parameters for Media. The platform architecture hosts event galleries, photographic media, and video recordings uploaded directly by the Organizer.
5.2. Organizer Liability. In the event the Organizer uploads multimedia containing the identifiable biometric or facial likeness of a Data Subject, the Organizer maintains the absolute legal status of Data Controller for that specific media. The Company processes this visual data strictly to facilitate the digital hosting services mandated by the Organizer.
5.3. Exercise of Rights Regarding Media. Any Data Subject seeking to exercise statutory rights under the PDPA—specifically including requests for the deletion, obfuscation, or blurring of event photographs—must direct such requests exclusively and directly to the relevant Organizer.
6. ARTIFICIAL INTELLIGENCE AND ALGORITHMIC TRANSPARENCY
6.1. Algorithmic Processing Authorization. The Company utilizes proprietary and third-party generative artificial intelligence algorithms strictly to synthesize educational records and generate professional resumes on behalf of the Data Subject.
6.2. Prohibition on Foundational Model Training. The Company expressly guarantees that the personally identifiable information of the Data Subject is strictly prohibited from being utilized to train, fine-tune, or enhance any foundational machine learning models or external third-party algorithms.
6.3. Output Verification Liability. All algorithmic outputs and generated content are provided strictly on an "as-is" basis. The Data Subject bears the absolute responsibility for reviewing, editing, and verifying the factual accuracy, completeness, and professional viability of the generated resumes prior to any academic or professional utilization.
7. SUB-PROCESSING AND DATA DISCLOSURE
The Company does not sell or lease Personal Data to third parties. Data disclosure is strictly limited to the following operational necessities:
7.1. Engagement of Sub-Processors. To ensure the continuous delivery and scalability of the Services, the Company engages trusted third-party cloud infrastructure providers and artificial intelligence application programming interfaces. All sub-processors are legally bound by strict data protection agreements that comply with the Personal Data Protection Act of Thailand.
7.2. Institutional Data Sharing. Platform analytics, attendance records, and credential verification metrics may be shared directly with the respective Organizer that issued the digital certificate, strictly for the purposes of institutional auditing and event administration.
7.3. Statutory Disclosures. The Company reserves the right to disclose Personal Data to regulatory authorities, law enforcement agencies, or judicial bodies exclusively when compelled by a valid legal mandate, subpoena, or statutory obligation under applicable law.
8. DATA SECURITY AND RETENTION PARAMETERS
8.1. Technical Security Measures. The Company implements and maintains comprehensive technical and organizational security measures, including data encryption and secure server architecture, designed to protect Personal Data against unauthorized access, accidental loss, alteration, or unlawful destruction.
8.2. Retention Period. The Company shall retain the foundational Account Data for the duration of the active account lifecycle. Data will be securely retained until the Data Subject formally requests account deletion via the platform interface, or until the termination of the data processing agreement with the associated Organizer.
8.3. Exemption for Claimed Credentials. Notwithstanding the aforementioned data destruction protocols, the Company shall not be obligated to locate, recall, or destroy digital certificates or algorithmic resumes that have been previously downloaded, transmitted, or independently stored by the Data Subject.
9. DATA SUBJECT RIGHTS AND EXERCISE MECHANISMS
9.1. Statutory Rights. Under the Personal Data Protection Act of Thailand (PDPA) and applicable data protection laws, the Data Subject retains the right to request access to, rectification of, or erasure of their Personal Data. Furthermore, the Data Subject retains the right to restrict processing, object to certain processing activities, and request data portability.
9.2. Account Data Requests. For Personal Data wherein the Company operates as the Data Controller (e.g., direct account registration data and platform-generated profiles), the Data Subject may exercise the aforementioned rights directly through the privacy settings within the platform interface or by submitting a formal written request to the designated Data Protection Officer of the Company.
9.3. Event Data Requests. For Personal Data wherein the Company operates strictly as a Data Processor (e.g., digital certificates, event attendance logs, and event photography), the Data Subject must direct all requests to exercise statutory rights directly to the respective Organizer. The Company shall not independently act upon such requests without the explicit, documented instruction of the Organizer.
10. CONTACT AND COMPLIANCE
The Company reserves the right to amend, update, or modify this Privacy Notice at its sole discretion to reflect changes in statutory legal obligations, technical infrastructure, or operational practices. The Company shall provide explicit notification of any material changes via the platform interface or electronic mail prior to such changes taking effect. Continued utilization of the Services following such notification constitutes acknowledgment of the revised Privacy Notice.
Any formal inquiries, complaints, or requests regarding this Privacy Notice or the data processing practices of the Company must be directed in writing to the designated Data Protection Officer at: support.medalverse@gmail.com.
